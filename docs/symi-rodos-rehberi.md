
# Symi & Rhodes Crossing Guide

This guide is prepared for captains and guests crossing from Fethiye to the neighboring islands of Rhodes and Symi.

## 1. Fethiye - Rhodes Ferry Line
Regular ferry services are operated from Fethiye Harbor to Rhodes.
*   **Trip Duration:** Approximately 45-50 minutes by fast catamaran.
*   **Operators:** Sky Marine, Turyol, Ocean Travel.
*   **Passport & Visa:** A valid Schengen visa or (when applicable) a "Gate Visa" is required. Green passport holders are exempt from visas.

## 2. Rhodes - Symi Crossing
Multiple services run daily from Rhodes to Symi.
*   **Trip Duration:** 50 minutes with fast ferries (Dodekanisos Seaways), 1.5 - 2 hours with classic large ships.
*   **Arrival Points:** Most ferries dock at the Symi Center (Gialos) port. Some services also stop at the Panormitis Monastery in the south.

## 3. Famous Bays of Symi Island
*   **Agios Georgios Dysalonas:** The most iconic bay of the island. It sits at the base of a vertical cliff about 300 meters high. Accessible only by sea.
*   **Nanou Bay:** Famous for its crystal clear water and pebble beach. It has a natural environment often visited by goats.
*   **Pedi Beach:** A quiet bay with a settlement, reachable on foot or by bus.
*   **Marathounda:** The water is very clear and it is generally known as one of the quietest bays on the island.
*   **Panormitis:** A sheltered southern bay housing a large monastery.

## 4. Gastronomy Stops in Symi (Ada Recommendations)
*   **Manos Fish Restaurant:** The most famous restaurant in Symi. Known for its celebrity photos on the walls and "Symi Shrimp."
*   **Odyssia Restaurant:** A select venue at the end of Gialos harbor known for fresh seafood and traditional Greek appetizers.
*   **The Tholos:** A high-quality restaurant overlooking the harbor, offering a more romantic and modern touch.
*   **Pantelis Restaurant:** A long-established family business in the harbor for traditional home cooking and fresh fish.

*Note: Symi shrimp (small, eaten with the shell) is the island's most important delicacy; don't leave without trying it.*
